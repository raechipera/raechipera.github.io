About

I'm a doctoral candidate at National University working on reservoir computing and nonlinear dynamics. My dissertation investigates fractal activation functions — Cantor, Weierstrass, Mandelbrot — in Echo State Networks, and what happens when you replace the usual smooth nonlinearities with something mathematically wilder.

Before this I did SIGINT in the Marine Corps, deployed to Iraq, trained in Arabic linguistics at DLI, and spent a while in quantitative finance. I also taught myself calculus in a national lab library, which is either a fun origin story or a cry for help depending on your perspective.

I also run Jaxorik AI Research Group, a WOSB/SDVOSB firm focused on explainable AI for disaster response and critical infrastructure.

Research

Beyond Lipschitz Continuity and Monotonicity: Fractal and Chaotic Activation Functions in Echo State Networks preprint
with J. Du & I. Tsapara · arXiv:2512.14675
Theoretical analysis of fractal activation functions in Echo State Networks, introducing the Degenerate Echo State Property (d-ESP) as a formal framework for characterizing reservoir dynamics under non-smooth, self-similar activations. Analytical work; empirical validation is the dissertation.
Dissertation: Beyond Smooth Activations: Irregular Functions for Modeling Chaotic Data Patterns in Neural Networks in progress
National University · Committee: Du, Tsapara, Dhou
Proposal defended April 2026. Full study examines Cantor, Weierstrass, and Mandelbrot activations across multiple benchmark datasets using MANOVA-based statistical design. The goal is a principled theoretical account of when and why fractals work in recurrent architectures.
